# Pastilla Paper Fillings (Thermomix)

Quick, crispy briouats and mini pastillas. Thermomix handles the chopping and sautéing.

> [!INFO] Recipe Info
> **Prep time:** 10-15 min per filling
> **Equipment:** Thermomix, pastilla paper
> **Makes:** Enough for ~12-15 pieces

---

## Classic Tuna, Vermicelli, and Olive Filling

Savory staple that comes together in minutes.

### Ingredients
- 100g dry vermicelli (glass noodles)
- 2 cans of tuna, thoroughly drained
- 1 medium onion, quartered
- Handful of fresh parsley and cilantro
- 50g pitted green olives
- 20g olive oil
- **Spices:** 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp sweet paprika, salt, pepper, dash of harissa (optional)

### Instructions
1. Soak the vermicelli in boiling water for 5 minutes. Drain completely and use scissors to cut the noodles into short pieces.
2. Place the onion, parsley, and cilantro into the Thermomix mixing bowl. Chop **5 sec / speed 5**. Scrape down the sides.
3. Add the olive oil and sauté **4 min / 120°C / speed 1**.
4. Add the olives and spices. Mix **5 sec / speed 4** to roughly chop the olives and incorporate the spices.
5. Transfer the warm mixture to a large mixing bowl. Add the drained tuna and the cut vermicelli.
6. Toss everything together until well combined.

## Quick Vegetable and Tuna Filling

More vegetables, soy sauce for deeper savory flavor.

### Ingredients
- 50g dry vermicelli (prepared as above)
- 1 can of tuna, drained
- 1 carrot, peeled and cut into chunks
- 1 zucchini, cut into chunks
- 1 small onion, quartered
- 20g olive oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- **Spices:** 1/2 tsp ground ginger, black pepper, pinch of salt (adjust based on soy sauce)

### Instructions
1. Place the carrot, zucchini, and onion into the Thermomix mixing bowl. Chop **5 sec / speed 5**. Scrape down the sides.
2. Add the olive oil. Sauté **5 min / 120°C / speed 1**.
3. Add the soy sauce, ginger, pepper, and salt. Mix **10 sec / speed 2**.
4. Transfer the cooked vegetables to a bowl. Gently fold in the drained tuna and the prepared vermicelli.

---

## Tips for Pastilla Paper

- **Cool completely:** Always let fillings cool down entirely before placing on the paper. Hot filling creates steam → soggy, torn paper.
- **Prevent drying:** Keep pastilla paper covered with a clean, slightly damp kitchen towel while working.
- **Seal the edges:** Use flour + water paste (or beaten egg yolk) to glue edges closed before baking/frying.
