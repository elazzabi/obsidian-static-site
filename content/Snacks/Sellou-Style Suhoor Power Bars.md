# Sellou-Style Suhoor Power Bars (Thermomix)

Honey-free energy bars inspired by traditional Sellou. Perfect for suhoor — filling, portable, and keeps you going.

> [!INFO] Recipe Info
> **Prep time:** 20 min
> **Chill time:** 2+ hours
> **Storage:** Up to 2 weeks in fridge
> **Makes:** ~12-16 bars

---

## Ingredients

- 150g raw almonds
- 50g toasted sesame seeds
- 100g rolled oats (traditional, not quick oats)
- 250g pitted dates (soft and sticky)
- 50g butter (or coconut oil)
- 1–2 tbsp orange blossom water (eau de fleur d'oranger) or warm water
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- ½ tsp ground anise seeds (nafaa — optional, for authentic flavor)
- 60g rice puffs

---

## Instructions

### Step 1: Roast the Almonds (Oven)

1. Preheat oven to **175°C** using top/bottom heat mode
2. Spread raw almonds in a single layer on an ungreased baking sheet
3. Roast on middle rack for **10–12 minutes**, stirring halfway
4. Remove when they smell deeply nutty and are golden-brown inside

> [!WARNING] Crucial
> Take them off the hot tray immediately and let them cool **completely** to room temperature before putting them in the Thermomix.

### Step 2: Prepare the Dry Mix (Thermomix)

1. Place completely cooled roasted almonds in the Thermomix bowl
2. Crush using **Turbo / 1 sec / 2 times** (chunky pieces, not powder)
3. Transfer chopped almonds to a large mixing bowl
4. Add 50g toasted sesame seeds and 100g rolled oats to the same bowl
5. Set aside

### Step 3: Make the Date Caramel Binder (Thermomix)

1. Place 250g dates, 50g butter, cinnamon, anise, and 1 tbsp orange blossom water into empty Thermomix bowl
2. Blend for **20 sec / Speed 6**
3. Scrape down the sides

> [!TIP] Hydration check
> If the mixture looks too dry to bind bars, add the second tablespoon of liquid.

4. Heat the paste: **3 min / 80°C / Speed 2**

### Step 4: Combine and Set (Thermomix + Manual)

1. Add reserved dry mix (almonds, sesame, oats) back into the warm date paste
2. Mix for **20 sec / Reverse / Speed 3**
3. Transfer to the large bowl
4. Add 60g rice puffs and **fold gently by hand** with a spatula

> [!TIP] Why by hand?
> Folding by hand keeps the puffs crunchy instead of turning them to dust.

5. Line a square baking tin with parchment paper
6. Pour mixture in and press down **firmly** (important for bars to hold without honey)
7. Refrigerate for at least **2 hours** to set

### Step 5: Cut and Store

1. Once firm, lift the block out using the parchment paper
2. Cut into bars
3. Store in an airtight container in the fridge
4. Place parchment paper between each bar so dates don't stick together
